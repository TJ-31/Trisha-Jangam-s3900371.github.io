Although these three tests are in different categories, they all gave quite accurate results about my character and personality. When I read the results of the three tests, some key points stated the exact characteristics that I thought I have. So, for me, these tests are like a way to look through myself as well as a way to improve some of my outgoing skills such as, in ’16 personalities test’, it mostly showed how introvert I am, which means I need to improve my communication skills as well as gain the ability to make decisions fast instead of thinking. Additionally, in the third test, ‘leadership styles, it stated that I am someone who “never blindly trusts” anyone. For a moment, I re-gained the memories of how I have never gone back to people who I have lost trust in and how I still behave like that. So, I found it interesting how this test exactly points this part of me in the leadership styles and how it is an advantageous characteristic for a leader.

Since the results started that I am an introvert person, when participating in a team, I may not be able to convey my interpretations or my ideas due to a lack of confidence and being an introverted person. Hence, this may impact the team about my behaviour because people may assume or perceive me as a person who has an “attitude”, leading me to develop a negative impression regarding my behaviour. 

When I form a team, I need to consider aspects such as communication, behaviour as well as my expressions because I think our expressions give out more trust and truth compared to our communication. Although it could be impossible to become perfect in all these areas, I will try to improve on myself so that I can one day probably become close to being perfect in these sectors.
